LIGHT GRAFFITI

MaxMSP, DMX Lighting, Illustrator

A spatial light installation built as a class project using Max/MSP to program and control DMX fixtures in real time. Custom patches were written to make the lights audio reactive, with color, intensity, and movement triggered live by the kick of the song, flooding the room in deep reds and warm ambers while casting dramatic shaped shadows across the walls. The project explored physical space as a medium, using sound as the engine and light as the output. The result was an atmosphere where every beat had a physical presence in the room, controlled entirely through code.

SPATIAL VISUALS

MaxMSP, Face Detection

A live audiovisual installation built as a class project, combining real-time generative visuals with a DJ performance to create an immersive dance environment. Responsive graphics were designed and triggered live using TouchDesigner, reacting to the energy of the space and the music as it unfolded. Bold typographic overlays, red-saturated lighting, and shifting visual layers transformed the room into a single cohesive sensory experience. The project explored the intersection of sound, movement, and real-time design, treating the audience not as passive viewers but as active participants shaping the atmosphere through their presence and motion.

DISSOCIATION

TouchDesigner, VR, 360° Camera

A class project built in TouchDesigner using footage captured with a 360 degree camera inside a live music venue. The equirectangular footage was mapped and processed into an interactive video installation where viewers can navigate the full space using their mouse, looking freely around the room as the performance unfolds. The project sits between documentary and experience design, translating a physical moment in time into something spatially explorable long after it ended. The result blurs the line between video and environment, giving the audience agency over how they witness the space.

BEDTIME STORIES

TouchDesigner, Projection Mapping, Audio-reactive

A personal project exploring what it means to live inside your music. Built in TouchDesigner, generative fluid visuals are projected directly onto the bedroom wall and react in real time to audio, shifting and breathing with the sound. The goal was to push past the idea of the bedroom as a static space and turn it into something alive, where the environment itself responds to what you are listening to. The result is an intimate audiovisual experience that transforms an ordinary room into something closer to a mood, one that changes every time you press play.

THE MIRROR

HTML/CSS/P5JS, ML5, Facial Emotion Detection

A class project built with HTML, CSS, and JavaScript that uses facial detection to turn a webcam into an affirmation mirror. When your expression is neutral or idle, the piece prompts you to smile. When it reads sadness or tension in your face, the screen wraps you in animated roses, sparkles, and the reminder that you did nothing wrong. The visual language is intentionally maximalist and tender, drawing on early web aesthetics to create something that feels warm rather than clinical. The project reframes the camera as a tool for care rather than surveillance, making technology respond to your emotional state with softness instead of data.
