RAVEU

Mobile App Prototype, UX/UI, Branding, Figma

A UX/UI concept for a mobile app designed to connect ravers with events, DJs, collectives, and venues in their city. The app covers the full user journey from event discovery and attendance to post-event reviews and social connection, with dedicated pages for DJs, collectives, locations, and friends. Privacy controls let users manage who sees their activity, while a story feature lets them share moments from the night. The standout feature is a built-in Harm Reduction screen, giving attendees quick access to a flashlight, alarm, and direct HR call at any point in the app. Designed in Figma with a fully linked prototype across multiple iterations, the project demonstrates end-to-end product thinking, a strong visual system in red and black, and a genuine understanding of the underground music community it serves.

DECKSDEEP

Web Design, UX/UI, Figma

A class project built in HTML, CSS, and JavaScript, DecksDeep is an immersive educational website tracing the full history and culture of DJing, from vinyl turntablism to modern digital mixing. The site is structured around three pillars: Analog, Mixer, and Digital, with the homepage designed to mirror the layout of an actual DJ setup, using spinning vinyl and CDJ icons as interactive navigation. The design system was built from scratch with a tight black, white, and pure red palette, custom iconography drawn from real equipment, and animation concepts where hovering images transform into playable video with audio. The process included landscape analysis, sitemapping, responsive wireframing across desktop, tablet, and mobile, multiple design directions, and a full set of high fidelity mockups. The result is a content-rich, visually confident web experience that treats DJing as the serious art form it is, and makes learning about it feel as dynamic as the music itself.

CONNECTURE CANADA

Client Work
Mobile App Prototype, UX/UI, Branding, Motion Graphics

A Figma web design for Connecture Canada, a French-language organization focused on connecting immigrant job seekers with Canadian employers. The design covers a full homepage including hero, About Us, Vision and Mission, partner logos, a main activities section showcasing community events, and a newsletter signup. The layout is warm and approachable, using soft pinks, deep reds, and a bold blue footer, with French-language copy throughout reflecting the organization's community. The design gives the client a credible, welcoming digital presence that clearly communicates their mission of breaking the cycle of poverty and building lasting connections between newcomers and opportunity.

ECULEO

Client Work
Mobile App Prototype, UX/UI, Branding, Motion Graphics

A mobile UI design for a bike rack locator app, built in Photoshop. The client needed a clean, functional interface that let cyclists quickly find nearby bike racks on a live map. The design covers the full user flow from a bold cycling photography splash screen through login and sign up, to an interactive map view with searchable locations pinned across the city. The visual system is confident and sporty, built around a deep purple palette with high contrast typography that feels at home in the cycling community. The result is a polished, ready-to-develop UI that gives the client a strong foundation to bring the app to life.

SYNSPACE

Web Design Prototype, UX/UI, Figma

A Figma prototype for a community blog platform built for people with synesthesia to share how they experience the world through blended senses. The user flow covers landing, category browsing, story threads, upload, and confirmation screens. Stories are organized through a colour-coded circle system tied to synesthesia categories, with privacy controls for anonymous posting and a tagging system for filtering by experience type. The visual identity leans into the subject matter with deep blacks, vivid saturated colour, and a cosmic Saturn motif that gives the platform a distinct, otherworldly feel.

SPOT MY LOVE

Mobile App Prototype, UX/UI, Branding, Figma

A Figma prototype for a music-based dating app that integrates Spotify to surface compatibility through listening habits. User profiles display top songs and most listened tracks alongside the standard dating app conventions of photos, age, and messaging. Matches can share songs directly in chat as a conversation starter. The project exists in two versions, a clean design and a deceptive version, exploring how UI affordances and motion can guide or mislead user behavior. The work sharpened an understanding of interaction design principles and how small interface decisions carry real consequences for trust and user experience.

W.RLD

Experience Design (XD), Web Design, Figma

A class project built in HTML, CSS, and JavaScript that uses experimental UI motion, VR, and sound to challenge how people consume information online. The site opens with large-scale kinetic typography asking questions about comprehension and truth, then layers live social media interfaces on top of one another to create a disorienting, overwhelming collage of simultaneous feeds. The experience is intentional: by stacking Instagram, X, and Pinterest in the same viewport, the site forces the user to confront the noise and contradiction baked into everyday digital life. Sound and VR elements deepen the immersion, pulling the viewer further into the environment rather than letting them observe it from a distance. The project sits at the intersection of web design, net art, and media criticism.

THE MIRROR

HTML/CSS/P5JS, ML5, Facial Emotion Detection

A class project built with HTML, CSS, and JavaScript that integrates facial detection to create an emotionally responsive web experience. The interface uses real-time expression recognition to trigger contextual feedback, prompting a smile when the user is idle and surfacing affirmations when sadness or tension is detected. The visual design leans into maximalist early web aesthetics, using layered animation and bold typography to create warmth rather than clinical utility. The project explores affective computing and emotionally intelligent UI, reframing the camera as a tool for care and pushing the boundaries of how interfaces can respond to human emotional states in real time.
